7. Which of these language options is correct for you?

Examples of proof that you have knowledge of English or French (for more details see section 15 of the guide):

  • Results from an IRCC approved third party language test
  • Diploma, certificate or final transcript demonstrating completion from a secondary or post-secondary school that was completed in English or French
  • Evidence of achieving CLB/NCLC 4 in a government funded language training program
  • If you are deaf: Supporting evidence of an audiogram along with an attestation issued by a Canadian audiologist

  • I am between 18 and 54 and I have proof that I have knowledge of English or French

Disclaimer: These questions are based on the general/legal requirements for a Canadian citizenship application under subsection 5(1) of the Citizenship Act. The results provide a general guideline to help you determine if you are ready to apply for Canadian citizenship and is not legal advice, nor will it be part of your formal application.
